# Statistical Analysis of Depopulation in Spain (2002–2020)

Bachelor's Thesis completed for the Applied Statistics Degree.

## Overview

This project analyzes demographic decline across Spanish provinces between 2002 and 2020 using statistical learning and panel data techniques.

The objective is to identify patterns of depopulation, distinguish provinces affected by long-term demographic decline from densely populated regions, and evaluate the socioeconomic factors associated with changes in population density over time.

## Dataset

The datasets were built using official public sources, primarily the Spanish National Statistics Institute (INE) and other governmental statistical agencies.

The study combines:

* Cross-sectional provincial data (2019–2020)
* Longitudinal panel data covering 2002–2020
* Demographic indicators
* Economic indicators
* Geographic variables

## Methodology

### Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Data exploration and statistical profiling were performed to understand the distribution, variability and relationships between demographic and economic indicators.

### Cluster Analysis

A Two-Step Cluster Analysis was applied to:

* Differentiate depopulated and highly populated regions
* Identify subgroups within depopulated provinces
* Detect structural differences between territories

### Panel Data Regression

Linear panel data models were used to analyze how demographic and socioeconomic variables influence population density over time while accounting for province-specific effects and temporal dynamics.

## Key Topics Analyzed

* Population density
* Population ageing
* Birth and mortality rates
* Employment and unemployment indicators
* Economic activity
* GDP per capita
* Household income
* Economic sector distribution
* Geographic characteristics

## Main Outcome

The analysis identifies distinct demographic profiles across Spanish provinces and highlights the variables most strongly associated with population decline, providing statistical evidence that may support future regional development and demographic policies.
